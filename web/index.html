How it works?

Top-down approach

We start by building something meaningful that can delight the kids right away and get them hooked to learning the details.

Curiosity-driven

Our teaching methodology encourages students to build and experiment, to ignite a curiosity that can keep them going on after the program is complete.

Deploy or die

Students deploy their first website/app in the first couple of classes. They learn by experimenting, improving, and deploying their own live app, which they can share with friends and family.

100% Free

The courses are taught by volunteers who are working professionals (software engineers) from all over the world.

What we teach?

Three modules

The curriculum is centered around helping students build and deploy three apps by themselves. Each module happens over 8 to 10 sessions of one hour each (~one month).

HTML, CSS & Javascript

Starting from how the internet works, we teach students enought HTML, CSS and JS to help them build and deploy webapps. In our top-down approach, we focus on helping them build end-to-end apps.

Deploy with GitHub Pages

Students deploy their first website/app in the first couple of classes. They learn by experimenting, improving, and deploying their own live app, which they can share with friends and family.

Learn on a mobile phone

We designed the curriculum to be doable on an Android phone. Work using an IDE (including GitHub integration) on your phone, no Laptop or Desktop needed.

For teachers

Flexible and few hours

Tell me more about Coding4All

We want to make computer education accessible to each student in India regardless of their parents' qualifications or financial background.

We create a cohort of 4 students from underprivileged backgrounds ranging between class 8 to class 11 and then arrange a teacher for them, preferably a working professional from the tech industry.

The main goal behind this program is to ignite curiosity in students about computers & the world of technology.

Breaking the shackles of poverty is a long and generational process with unlimited & uncontrollable moving parts. Nobody can guarantee that. But the least we can do is to give access to students to computer programming, so they may consider opting for a computer graduation course after the 12th. Apart from engineering, there are other degree courses also available in computer science. And it is well established that computer programming knowledge leads to better career prospects.

This course could be a stepping stone.

This program is made for teaching computer programming to underprivileged students only. We ensure that all the selected students are comfortable with normal English and are interested in learning computer programming. We want your efforts to go in the right direction and towards meaningful change.

Side note - If this is your first time working with high school students, one thing you need to keep in mind is that while most of the students are expressive and respond in the class, there are always a few quiet and shy ones. So, don’t get bogged down. In our experience, they also come along once a couple of classes are done.

We also take feedback from teachers & students during the mid-course and implement changes if any.

Tell me about teaching

Teach enough HTML & version control to students that empower and encourage them to create their own websites, make changes at will and share with their friends and family.

At the end of the course, students will learn to create their own static HTML website with basic CSS and host them via Github pages.

The core philosophy behind the Coding4All course designing is the Top-Down approach. We believe that our main purpose behind this program is to ignite curiosity in students about computers and programming.

We put very high emphasis on relatable, measurable, and publishable outcomes after every module. And that is the reason we prioritize website deployment against few of the other theoretical things. In fact, this current course is not about programming and more about computers, tools, websites, and empowering kids to express their own creativity.

That is the reason, during this course, we encourage teachers to only focus student attention on exactly what students “need to know” at that particular stage.

As stated above, we will be following the Top-Down approach for our course. That means students don’t need to really know the full version control or all the operations. They only need to know the basic operations that empower them to deploy the code to their websites and make changes.

In our experience, students really enjoy making their personal websites, making changes at will, and demonstrating the same to their friends and family. We want to enable and encourage this behavior. That’s why Github. Plus Github pages make hosting very easy.

How much time do I have to commit?

The scope of the first module, as stated above is to create a static HTML website and deploy it to Github pages.

The first module will have 8 sessions of 1 to 1.5 hours over 4 weekends (Saturday/Sunday). So total commitment from your side for the first module is around 10-12 hours.

There are 3 modules in total. The second module covers basic javascript and the third module covers an introduction to databases using Airtable.

No. You don’t have to commit to all three modules. If you want to discontinue after the first module (creating and deploying a static HTML website), that’s completely fine. You can always resume at a later stage with the next modules if you feel like it.

We are in this for a long and we want you to enjoy this.

So, typically any of the following tracks are fine with us.

Option-1 You only want to commit to the first module (8 sessions and 10 to 12 hours). You will discontinue after this and will resume later only if you feel like it. No hard commitments.

Option-2 You will finish the first module. Take an optional break for a month or two (or maybe more). Continue with the second module with the same set of students whom you taught in the first module OR start afresh and teach the first module to a new set of students.

We want to give you as much flexibility as possible. Also, there will always be breaks between the modules due to students’ exams or teachers’ other personal commitments.

Breaks between modules are healthy and we encourage that.

If you wish to continue with us after the first module, we suggest that you continue with the same set of students as there would be then a comfort level, familiarity, and long-term emotional commitment.

Don’t worry. We will help you with the study material/videos and walk you through the syllabus. Also, our existing teachers’ community will also be there to help you out at any stage.

Someone from Coding4All can also join you for the first few classes.

No problem. We will work out the timings. We have teachers based out of North America conducting classes.
